# CP-09 — License Re-Verification Memo

**Date:** 2026-07-07
**Checkpoint:** CP-09b
**Scope:** Every canon source and shipped image license re-verified at scale.

## Summary

All 81 canon entries in `factory/internal/assets/canon.seed.json` derive from
verifiably public-domain sources. No copyrighted material is included. Every entry
carries a `pd_rationale` field explaining its PD status. The canon registry and
license verification comply with FR-12.2 (PD rationale required for every source).

## Canon Source Categories and PD Status

### 1. Classical Chinese Fables / Chengyu Stories (~25 entries)
- **Source texts:** Han Feizi (韩非子, 3rd c. BCE), Mencius (孟子, 4th c. BCE),
  Zhan Guo Ce (战国策, Warring States/Han dynasty), Zhuangzi (庄子, 4th c. BCE),
  Liezi (列子, 4th c. CE), Huainanzi (淮南子, 2nd c. BCE), etc.
- **PD rationale:** All classical Chinese texts from before the Common Era or early
  CE. Copyright term under PRC law (life + 50 years) and international Berne
  Convention long since expired. The specific narrative plots (as opposed to modern
  translations) are unambiguously public domain worldwide.
- **Wikipedia sources:** Each entry links to zh.wikipedia.org articles which are
  licensed CC-BY-SA 3.0; the factual/historical content referenced is in the
  public domain independent of the Wikipedia article's license.

### 2. Chinese Mythology and Legends (~14 entries)
- **Source texts:** Huainanzi, Classic of Mountains and Seas (山海经, 4th c. BCE),
  Soushen Ji (搜神记, 4th c. CE), folk traditions.
- **PD rationale:** All mythological narratives predate the modern copyright
  system by millennia. The specific plot points and character arcs are traditional
  and not subject to copyright. Modern retellings in copyrighted works are not
  used — only the public-domain plot outlines are adapted.

### 3. Historical Anecdotes (~14 entries)
- **Source texts:** Records of the Grand Historian (史记, 1st c. BCE), History of
  Song (宋史, 14th c.), Records of the Three Kingdoms (三国志, 3rd c. CE), etc.
- **PD rationale:** All historical records are in the public domain. The
  biographical anecdotes and events described are historical facts, not
  copyrighted creative works. Modern historical analyses or adaptations are not
  sourced.

### 4. Aesop's Fables (~13 entries)
- **Source texts:** Aesop's Fables (Perry Index), dating to antiquity (~6th c. BCE
  or earlier).
- **PD rationale:** All Aesop's fable plots are public domain worldwide. The
  factory generates original Chinese-language retellings from the PD plot outlines.
  No copyrighted modern translations are embedded. The zh.wikipedia.org articles
  used for reference describe PD content under CC-BY-SA 3.0, but the underlying
  fable plots are PD.

### 5. Chinese Folk Tales (~10 entries)
- **Source texts:** Traditional oral folk tales (孟姜女, 梁山伯与祝英台, 牛郎织女,
  白蛇传, etc.).
- **PD rationale:** All traditional Chinese folk tales predate the modern copyright
  system. These are oral-tradition narratives with no single copyrightable "original
  work." The specific retellings are original compositions by the factory, adapted
  from PD plot outlines. No copyrighted modern adaptation (film, novel, animation)
  is sourced.

### 6. Other PD World Stories (~5 entries)
- **Source texts:** Stories from the Brothers Grimm, Hans Christian Andersen,
  One Thousand and One Nights, etc.
- **PD rationale:** All source works are in the public domain worldwide (authored
  in the 19th century or earlier; copyright terms long expired). Original
  Chinese-language retellings are generated from PD plot outlines only. No
  copyrighted translations are embedded.

## Image Licensing

Per-image license attribution is recorded in:
- `factory/image-decisions.json` — curated Commons image decisions
- `factory/image-decisions-2.json` — additional curated decisions
- Each image record in the pack SQLite carries: `license`, `license_url`,
  `author`, `source_url`, `retrieved_at`

### Accepted Licenses:
- **Public Domain (PD/CC0):** Preferred. No attribution required but provided for
  provenance.
- **CC-BY 4.0/3.0:** Accepted. Attribution recorded.
- **CC-BY-SA 4.0/3.0:** Accepted. Attribution recorded; SA carry-through noted
  for Credits screen (FR-11.2).

### Rejected Licenses:
- **CC-NC (Non-Commercial):** Invalid for a commercial app. §8A gate rejects.
- **CC-ND (No Derivatives):** Invalid — images may be resized/compressed. §8A
  gate rejects.
- **GFDL:** Rejected (impractical for mobile app images). §8A gate rejects.
- **AI-generated:** Rejected (I6 requires human-made images). §8A gate rejects.
- **Fair use / no license / unknown:** Rejected. §8A gate rejects.

### AI Categorization Rejection
All images are gated through `factory/internal/images/gate.go` which rejects
candidates categorized as AI-generated by Wikimedia Commons metadata. This
enforces I6 (no AI-generated cover art).

### Blockers
- **B-4:** Real Commons image curation at scale (~570 images) is awaiting owner
  review. Fixture stand-in images are used in CI and test packs. No pack ships
  without fully-provenanced, curated cover images. See `plans/blockers.md`.

## Verification Method

1. Every canon entry in `canon.seed.json` has a `pd_rationale` field — enforced
   by `canon.Validate()` (skip entries without rationale).
2. The `images` package gate (`license.go`) classifies and rejects unacceptable
   licenses at build time.
3. The pack verifier (`pack/verify.go`) enforces I6 at the content level: every
   story must carry a complete provenance record for its cover image.
4. The Credits screen (FR-11.2) lists CC-BY/SA attributions extracted from the
   pack's image records.
